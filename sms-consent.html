SMS Consent / Opt-In

Last updated: March 24, 2026

Austin Broyles sends SMS messages only to individuals who provide express verbal consent directly to receive them.

How Consent Is Collected

Before any SMS is sent, Austin clearly asks for permission using the following verbal disclosure:

"May I send you text messages from Austin Broyles for scheduling, appointment confirmations, and service-related follow-up at this mobile number? Message frequency varies based on your interaction with me. Message and data rates may apply. Reply STOP to opt out and HELP for help. I will not share your mobile number with third parties for marketing or promotional purposes. You can review the Privacy Policy at https://ahbroyles.github.io/privacy and Terms at https://ahbroyles.github.io/terms. Do you agree to receive these text messages?"

SMS messages are sent only after the recipient clearly says "yes" or otherwise provides direct affirmative verbal consent. All messages originate from (817) 859-7966.

Consent Confirmation

Following verbal consent, Austin sends a single confirmation SMS from (817) 859-7966 confirming the opt-in and restating opt-out instructions. Example confirmation message:

"You're confirmed to receive texts from Austin Broyles for scheduling, appointment confirmations, and service-related follow-up. Message and data rates may apply. Message frequency varies. Reply STOP to opt out or HELP for help."

Consent Records

Austin maintains a private log of each consent interaction, including the recipient's name, phone number, date of consent, and the context in which consent was obtained. These records are retained to verify opt-in status upon request.

No Purchased or Scraped Lists

Austin Broyles does not use purchased lists, rented lists, scraped numbers, or unsolicited marketing lists. SMS is used only for conversational and transactional purposes, including scheduling, appointment confirmations, and service-related follow-up.

Opt Out

Recipients may reply STOP at any time to opt out or reply HELP for help.
